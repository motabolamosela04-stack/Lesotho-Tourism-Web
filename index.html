Lesotho, known as the Kingdom in the Sky, is a mountainous country entirely surrounded by South Africa, with most land above 1,400 meters. This elevation shapes its climate, biodiversity, and tourism appeal, making it ideal for eco-tourism and adventure travel. The country offers untouched landscapes and authentic cultural experiences that differentiate it from mass tourism destinations. Tourism development focuses on sustainability and community participation to ensure long-term benefits. The sector contributes to economic diversification and employment creation. As a result, Lesotho continues to grow as a niche tourism destination (World Bank, 2023).

Lesotho offers breathtaking mountain landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and unique highland experiences. Visitors can explore iconic attractions such as Maletsunyane Falls, one of Africa’s highest single-drop waterfalls, or enjoy scenic drives through the dramatic Sani Pass. Cultural tourism is also significant, with Thaba Bosiu representing the historical heart of the Basotho nation. Adventure tourism activities such as hiking, pony trekking, and dam tours at Katse Dam continue to attract both domestic and international tourists. Activities such as hiking, pony trekking, and cultural exploration highlight the country’s uniqueness. Community-based tourism ensures local participation and equitable economic benefits. This aligns with global sustainable tourism trends that prioritize environmental conservation. Infrastructure improvements are enhancing accessibility and visitor satisfaction. These developments strengthen Lesotho’s position in the regional tourism market. Consequently, tourism remains a key driver of growth (Government of Lesotho, 2021).
